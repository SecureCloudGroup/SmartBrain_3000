Direction: calm precision-minimal — privacy as aesthetic. Everything here runs on the proposed token system (tokens.css); on approval those tokens become the app's, verbatim. The switcher at the bottom of every page flips theme, accent candidate, and the desktop-nav variant — your choice follows you across pages.

The eight questions this review answers

  1. Accent: A (muted teal — the calm/secure statement) or B (slate-indigo — continuity with today)? Same answer in both themes?
  2. Desktop nav: sidebar rail, or the refined top bar? (Toggle on the chat page.)
  3. Mobile tabs: is Chat · Knowledge · Activity · More the right four? Should the tab bar hide while the keyboard is open?
  4. Chat rows: full-width messages (no bubbles) — comfortable? Is the ~740px column right?
  5. ActionCard: does it read “deliberate” without being alarming? Default-deny for irreversible actions OK?
  6. Palettes: dark #121212 family and warm-white light — right darkness/warmth?
  7. Header identity: the “SB” mark shown here, or a mascot-derived mark? (Tray monogram stays either way.)
  8. Muscle memory: anything that must NOT move?

Reply in the PR (or dictate changes in chat) — target is ≤2 review rounds, then tokens.css locks and implementation starts.

What deliberately did NOT change

The copy voice (already the strongest brand asset), the approval-gate model, all information architecture and features, the trust guarantees. This is a re-skin of how the product looks and feels — nothing about what it does.
